Ongoing research · BioSensors Lab

Physics-informed polarization geolocalization

A synthetic-data and hierarchical learning pipeline for predicting solar-elevation trajectories and regressing geolocation from polarization imagery. The project combines physics priors with deep transformers to scale training without depending solely on costly field collection.

  • Modeled solar-elevation dependencies to generate scalable synthetic polarization data.
  • Combined a Vision Transformer with a two-stage PointTransformer for precise regression.
  • Reached sub-0.5 degree latitude and longitude error on meaningful fractions of test days.

SUNLAB · 2025

Multi-modal synthetic EHR with medical image generation

A conditional diffusion system for synthesizing clinically plausible medical images from structured visit representations, while using graph neural networks to model sparse dependencies across patient demographics and codes.

  • Designed conditioning signals from multi-modal patient visit structure.
  • Added a GNN to capture latent dependencies between sparse medical features.
  • Framed the pipeline around Digital Twin and External Control Arm use cases.

Intelligent Motion Lab · 2024

Robust 3D head pose estimation for visual servoing

A robotics perception pipeline for robust head pose estimation under occlusion and extreme angles, combining 3DDFA landmarks, an Unscented Kalman Filter, and precise eye-to-hand and eye-in-hand calibration on a shared UR5 control stack.

  • Integrated custom vision modules into a real-time visual servoing system.
  • Added dynamic inter-landmark constraints to stabilize pose tracking with a UKF.
  • Validated the pipeline on the RGB-D BiWi Kinect dataset.

Sharif University of Technology · 2019–2023

Wearable rehabilitation intelligence

A multi-year research thread on inertial human activity recognition for Parkinson's disease and telerehabilitation, covering data augmentation, sensor placement, deep sequence models, and visualization-based validation.

  • Reduced an HAR system to two sensors while preserving 95% recall on healthy subjects.
  • Improved ConvLSTM accuracy from 92% to 98% using targeted augmentation methods.
  • Benchmarked multi-task CNN-BiLSTM models across 65 activities and 43 subjects.
