Home / 3D head pose estimation

Intelligent Motion Lab · Jan 2024–Oct 2024

Stabilizing head pose in a robot loop where occlusion and geometry both matter.

This project sits squarely between computer vision and robotics systems work. The goal was not only to estimate head pose, but to make that estimate reliable enough to support real-time visual servoing on a UR5 setup with multiple cameras and endoscopes.

Vision + control Pose estimation that survives enough noise to drive robot behavior.

Pipeline

From landmarks to a stable control signal

  • Used 3DDFA as the facial landmark detector for head pose estimation.
  • Built an Unscented Kalman Filter with dynamic inter-landmark constraints to absorb failures from occlusion and extreme angles.
  • Performed eye-to-hand and eye-in-hand calibration for a multi-camera system with two depth cameras and four endoscopes.
  • Integrated the perception module into a shared UR5 visual servoing stack.

Validation

  • Validated on the RGB-D BiWi Kinect dataset.
  • Presented as a computer vision term project.
  • Focused on robustness instead of only nominal accuracy.

Stack

3DDFA UKF Calibration UR5
Problem framing

Raw landmark detectors are not enough when the head rotates strongly or parts of the face disappear from view. The filter layer exists to convert unstable measurements into a trackable state estimate.

Systems contribution

The hidden difficulty is calibration: the pose estimator only becomes useful when every camera frame, robot frame, and control frame agree closely enough to support a closed-loop setup.
